Here is the complete brand identity built with deep respect for every faith. The core design decision is this: the logo contains no religious symbols at all — only light, cosmos, and language.

Why this approach respects everyone equally
The single most sensitive thing any multi-faith brand can do is accidentally elevate one tradition's symbols above another. So the NoorJyoti identity uses only three things that are universally sacred and owned by no single faith:

Light — every scripture from the Quran's Ayat-ul-Kursi to John 8:12 in the Bible to the Gita's description of the Atma uses light as its central metaphor for the divine. Using the word for light in 12 languages simultaneously means every faith sees itself reflected equally.

The cosmos — concentric orbits, rays, and a central flame appear in Islamic geometric art, Hindu mandalas, Buddhist thangkas, Jewish Kabbalistic diagrams, and medieval Christian illuminated manuscripts. No one tradition owns the circle or the star.

Gold and deep night — these are the only two colours present in every tradition's sacred manuscripts across 3,000 years of human history.

What was deliberately avoided
No Om, no Cross, no Crescent and Star, no Khanda, no Star of David, no Dharma Wheel appears anywhere in the mark — because placing any one of these as the central symbol, even with others alongside it, creates a hierarchy. The absence of all of them together is itself the statement: here, all are equal.
